# Evaluation Methodology

## Overview

The evaluation investigates the usability and effectiveness of a **3D XR interface for spatial audio design**.

The study focuses on how users interact with virtual loudspeakers and audio parameter controls in an immersive environment, and whether the proposed interface improves spatial understanding and workflow efficiency compared to traditional approaches.

The evaluation follows a **user-centered design methodology**, combining qualitative and quantitative measures.

---

## Evaluation Goals

The study aims to:

- assess usability of the XR spatial audio interface  
- evaluate spatial comprehension of audio source positioning  
- measure perceived efficiency of interaction workflows  
- analyze user confidence and satisfaction  
- identify design limitations and improvement opportunities  

---

## Prototype Fidelity

The evaluation is conducted using a **functional mid-fidelity prototype**.

The prototype includes:

- interactive speaker positioning  
- gain control interface  
- 6-band equalizer interaction  
- reverb parameter controls  
- real-time spatial audio feedback  
- save/load functionality  

Visual design is simplified, focusing on interaction clarity rather than aesthetic refinement.

---

## Participants

### Target Group

Participants are selected based on:

- basic familiarity with audio or music production  
- interest in immersive technologies  
- mixed levels of XR experience  

### Sample Size

Recommended sample size:

- **6–10 participants**

This range allows identification of major usability issues while remaining feasible within the thesis timeframe.

---

## Test Environment

The evaluation is conducted using:

- XR headset (e.g. Meta Quest)  
- headphones or loudspeaker monitoring  
- controlled indoor testing space  

Participants receive:

- a short introduction  
- demonstration of interaction techniques  
- opportunity for brief free exploration  

---

## Tasks

Participants are asked to complete structured tasks.

### Task 1 — Speaker Positioning

- Move a virtual speaker to a specified spatial location.
- Evaluate perceived accuracy and ease of movement.

### Task 2 — Gain Adjustment

- Balance the level of multiple speakers.
- Assess control precision and feedback clarity.

### Task 3 — Equalizer Modification

- Apply spectral changes to improve perceived clarity of a sound.
- Observe understanding of fixed frequency bands.

### Task 4 — Reverb Adjustment

- Modify environmental parameters to simulate different acoustic spaces.

### Task 5 — Scene Reconstruction

- Load a saved configuration and identify differences.

---

## Data Collection Methods

### Observational Data

- completion time  
- number of errors  
- hesitation or confusion  
- interaction strategies  

### Questionnaire Data

Participants complete a post-test questionnaire using **Likert-scale ratings**.

Example categories:

- ease of learning  
- spatial understanding  
- interaction comfort  
- perceived control precision  
- overall satisfaction  

---

## Example Questionnaire Items

Participants rate statements from **1 (strongly disagree) to 5 (strongly agree)**.

- I could easily understand where sound sources were located.
- Moving speakers felt natural and intuitive.
- Adjusting audio parameters was straightforward.
- The interface helped me experiment with spatial sound.
- I felt confident using the system.
- The auditory feedback was helpful.
- The interface was visually clear.
- I would use such a tool in real audio design work.

Open-ended questions:

- What was the most confusing aspect?
- What interaction felt most natural?
- What improvements would you suggest?

---

## Analysis Approach

### Quantitative Analysis

- calculate mean and standard deviation of Likert responses  
- compare task completion times  
- identify recurring usability bottlenecks  

### Qualitative Analysis

- thematic coding of participant feedback  
- identification of usability patterns  
- interpretation of spatial cognition observations  

---

## Evaluation Metrics

Key metrics include:

- task success rate  
- interaction time  
- perceived workload  
- spatial comprehension  
- subjective satisfaction  

These metrics support conclusions regarding interface effectiveness.

---

## Limitations

Potential limitations of the study:

- small participant sample size  
- prototype-level visual design  
- limited training time  
- variability in XR experience  
- controlled testing conditions  

These factors should be acknowledged when interpreting results.

---

## Expected Outcomes

The evaluation aims to determine whether:

- spatial interaction improves user understanding of audio layouts  
- direct manipulation reduces reliance on abstract parameter thinking  
- XR interfaces can support exploratory audio workflows  
- human-centered design principles enhance usability in spatial audio tools  

---

## Future Evaluation Extensions

Future studies could include:

- comparison with traditional desktop audio software  
- longitudinal learning studies  
- collaborative multi-user interaction testing  
- performance analysis in complex spatial scenes  
- expert user testing with professional sound designers  

---

## Summary

The evaluation provides structured insight into the usability and effectiveness of the proposed XR spatial audio interface.

By combining:

- task-based testing  
- subjective feedback  
- observational data  

the study supports informed conclusions about interaction design and future system development.
